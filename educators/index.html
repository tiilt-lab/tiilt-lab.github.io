For Educators

Everything below was designed with teachers and tested with real students. All of it is free to use in your classroom or out-of-school program. If you try any of it, we would love to hear how it went: tiiltlab@gmail.com.

SportSense for Data Literacy (Grade 5)

An 8-week sports-and-data curriculum in which students learn data processes through their own physical movement, using the Play Impossible Gameball, micro:bit, and Scratch. Each week includes a full lesson plan.

  1. Week 1 — What do sensors do? / Power of Sensors
  2. Week 2 — Why do sensors matter? / Sensor Enabled Play
  3. Week 3 — What do we use data for? / Getting to Know the micro:bit
  4. Week 4 — How can we use patterns in data? / Scratch for Body and Facial Recognition
  5. Week 5 — How do these parts work together? / Asking and Answering Questions
  6. Week 6 — Final Projects: Student Designing (Part 1)
  7. Weeks 7/8 — Final Projects: Student Designing (Part 2)

Background on the curriculum design is on the SportSense for Data Literacy project page.

Data in Motion (youth workshops)

Six discussion-and-activity lessons connecting sports, wearables, and data science, built for youth sports and out-of-school settings. Each page includes videos, discussion prompts, and activities.

  1. The Power of Sensors
  2. The Promise of Wearables
  3. Take Off that Silly Watch
  4. Designers Wanted
  5. Limitations with Wearables and Sensors
  6. Design Your Future

Tools

  1. TIILT Scratch — our variant of Scratch with micro:bit accelerometer support, used across the curricula above. Runs in the browser, nothing to install.
  2. micro:bit codes and games — open-source repositories used in our activities.
  3. micro:bit hex file for connecting a micro:bit to TIILT Scratch.
